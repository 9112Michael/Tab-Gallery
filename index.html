Virginia Frances Sterrett

American Illustrator (1900-1931)

Virginia Frances Sterrett was an American illustrator from Chicago who produced illustrations for three books before losing her life to tuberculosis. The images she produced are among the best of their type from the Art Nouveau period.

Two of the books are available in digital form and one is still protected by copyright and must be either bought or borrowed.

  • Tanglewood Tales
  • Old French Fairy Tales
  • Arabian Nights

Illustrator

Life story

Born in the rapidly growing metropolis of Chicago, Illinois in 1900, Virginia spent much of her youth drawing. Following the death of her father, she moved with her remaining family to Missouri. When she was around 13 years old she was awarded several prizes at the Kansas State fair for her drawings.
